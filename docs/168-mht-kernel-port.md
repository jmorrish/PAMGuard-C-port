# MHT Kernel Port

Date: 2026-07-10

## Purpose

The multi-hypothesis tracking kernel is the heart of PAMGuard's modern click train detector: it maintains every plausible grouping of detections as a pruned hypothesis tree. This slice ports `MHTKernel`/`TrackBitSet` and pins the kernel mechanics with a fixture generated by driving the **real Java `MHTKernel`** through its public interface.

## Reference semantics ported

`cpp-engine/include/pamguard/detectors/MhtKernel.h` (header-only, generic over the detection type):

- Branch growth: every existing hypothesis splits into include/exclude branches for each new detection (first detection seeds one-bit true/false tracks), chi2 objects cloned per branch and updated afterwards.
- Pruning (after `nPruneBackStart` detections): hypotheses stable-sorted by chi2 (`Collections.sort` semantics), then up to `nHold` rounds of: take the best branch; compare prefixes up to `kcount - nPruneback`; equal-prefix branches survive, intersecting branches are removed; branches whose coast count reaches `maxCoast` (or junk-flagged, or on final confirm-all) are confirmed with their bitset trimmed by one detection (untrimmed on confirm-all) and all intersecting branches removed.
- The all-coasts branch is re-added whenever absent; confirmed tracks accumulate; `confirmRemainingTracks` runs a pruneback-zero confirm-all pass.
- `MhtBitset` replicates `java.util.BitSet` content semantics (reads beyond length are false; equality and intersection ignore trailing zeros; `get(0, to)` prefixes).
- Kernel parameters match `MHTKernelParams` defaults: `nHold` 20, `nPruneback` 4, `nPruneBackStart` 5, `maxCoast` 3.

## Fixture

`MhtKernelFixtureExporter.java` drives the real kernel with a deterministic test chi2 implemented identically on both sides (IDI smoothness over included units + 0.1 per exclusion; coasts = trailing exclusions). Two scenarios — a steady 8-click train followed by widely spaced stragglers, and two interleaved trains — record per-step possibility/confirmed counts and the final confirmed tracks (exact bitset strings, chi2, cardinality). The fixture shows possibility growth capping at 32/48 under pruning and gap-forced confirmation, and `mht_kernel_parity` replays every row exactly (chi2 within 1e-12).

## Claim boundary

The kernel is pinned against a test chi2, not `StandardMHTChi2` (658 lines: chi2-variable combination, track-quality bonuses, coast handling, electrical noise filters), which remains the next MHT slice along with the bearing/correlation chi2 variables and wiring MHT into the session as an alternative to the max-ICI tracker. `clearKernelGarbage` (memory reclamation for long runs) is not yet ported.
