# LSQ Bearing Localiser Port

Date: 2026-07-03

## Purpose

Continues the bearing-localiser parity priority after the pair localiser port (`docs/157`). PAMGuard's `LSQBearingLocaliser` is the reference weighted least-squares bearing estimator for multi-element arrays; its semantics — fit weights, Jama QR solve, angle conventions, and the curvature-based error estimate — were not represented in the C++ engine. This slice ports them and pins them with a fixture generated by the real Jama and PamVector classes.

## Reference semantics ported

- Fit weights per pair: `(|v| / (unit(v) . errorVec))^2`, applied to both the matrix rows (`v/c * w`) and the right-hand side (`-delay * w`).
- Least-squares solve via a replica of Jama's Householder QR without pivoting, including Jama's scaled `hypot` (not `std::hypot`) and Jama's exact-zero `Rdiag` rank test.
- Angle convention: `azimuth = pi/2 - atan2(x, y)` (heading style), `elevation = asin(z)` after normalising the solution vector.
- Curvature-based error loop: twenty iterations with `aDiff` from 1 to 20 degrees; the reported errors come from the final (20 degree) iteration, exactly as PAMGuard leaves them.
- `logLikelihood` reproduces PAMGuard's per-pair `chi` overwrite (the last pair's contribution is used rather than a sum). Where the resulting curvature is negative, the error is `NaN` — visible in the fixture and matched exactly.

## Rank-deficiency findings

Two structural facts pinned during fixture design:

- Any 3-hydrophone pair set is rank deficient: the third baseline is exactly the difference of the other two, so the pair matrix spans at most two dimensions and Jama's solve throws `Matrix is rank deficient`. PAMGuard's LSQ localiser needs at least four non-coplanar hydrophones (line/pair geometries are `PairBearingLocaliser` / grid-localiser territory, per `BearingLocaliserSelector`).
- Collinear or coplanar 4-hydrophone sets fail the same way. The C++ port reports no result where Jama throws, and the checker pins the collinear 3-hydrophone case.

## Fixture

`LsqBearingFixtureExporter.java` drives the real Jama `QRDecomposition`/`Matrix` and `pamMaths.PamVector` from the PAMGuard classpath; only `prepare()`'s array-manager-derived inputs (hydrophone positions, per-pair separation error vectors, sound speed and errors) are supplied as case constants. Four cases: `volumetric-4ch`, `towed-4ch` (elongated, near-line cluster), `volumetric-4ch-alt-weights` (different weights/sound speed/timing error), and `wide-aperture-4ch`. The C++ check mirrors the catalogue by name, verifies the delay-count guard and the rank-deficiency behaviour, and compares azimuth/elevation/errors within `1e-9` with exact NaN matching.

## Validation

- `lsq_bearing_basic_parity` passes across all four cases.
- Full CTest suite passes `50/50` on Windows Release.

## Claim boundary

`prepare()`-side array derivation (array shape detection, separation error vectors from the hydrophone locator) is supplied as configuration. `MLGridBearingLocaliser`/simplex localisers remain unported. The service does not yet expose LSQ bearing outputs; wiring bearing localiser selection into the click pipeline is a follow-up slice.
